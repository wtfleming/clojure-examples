<td style="color:#000;">
<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px">
<div style="float:left;margin:0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0;"><a href="/wiki/File:RwandaVolcanoAndLake.jpg" class="image" title="A photograph of northwestern Rwanda"><img alt="A photograph of northwestern Rwanda" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/RwandaVolcanoAndLake.jpg/100px-RwandaVolcanoAndLake.jpg" width="100" height="151" /></a></div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a></b> is a country in central and eastern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> located a few degrees south of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">Equator</a>, bordered by <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burundi" title="Burundi">Burundi</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a>. All of Rwanda is at high elevation, with a geography dominated by mountains in the west, <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a> in the east, and numerous lakes throughout the country. The climate is temperate. The predominantly rural population of 11.7 million people forms three main groups: the <a href="/wiki/Hutu" title="Hutu">Hutu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tutsi" title="Tutsi">Tutsi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes_Twa" title="Great Lakes Twa">Twa</a>. After Rwanda was first settled by <a href="/wiki/Hunter_gatherer" title="Hunter gatherer" class="mw-redirect">hunter-gatherers</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Stone_Age" title="Stone Age">Stone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Ages</a>, the population coalesced into clans and then into a Tutsi-led <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Rwanda" title="Kingdom of Rwanda">kingdom</a>. It was colonised by Europeans in the 19th century and gained independence from Belgium on 1 July 1962 after a Hutu revolt led to massacres of Tutsis and the establishment of a Hutu-dominated republic. In 1990 the Tutsi-led <a href="/wiki/Rwandan_Patriotic_Front" title="Rwandan Patriotic Front">Rwandan Patriotic Front</a> (RPF) launched a <a href="/wiki/Rwandan_Civil_War" title="Rwandan Civil War">civil war</a>, which was followed by the 1994 <a href="/wiki/Rwandan_Genocide" title="Rwandan Genocide">Rwandan Genocide</a>, in which Hutu extremists killed an estimated 500,000 to 1 million Tutsi and moderate Hutu but were ultimately defeated by the RPF. The economy suffered during the genocide, but has since strengthened and depends heavily on subsistence agriculture. (<b><a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">more...</a></b>)</p>

<ul>
<li style="-moz-float-edge: content-box"><a href="/wiki/1770" title="1770">1770</a> <b><a href="/wiki/Lexell%27s_Comet" title="Lexell's Comet">Lexell's Comet</a></b> passed closer to the Earth than any other <a href="/wiki/Comet" title="Comet">comet</a> in recorded history, approaching to a distance of <span class="nowrap">0.015 <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_unit" title="Astronomical unit">AU</a></span>.</li>

<li style="-moz-float-edge: content-box"><a href="/wiki/1915" title="1915">1915</a> <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>: German fighter pilot <b><a href="/wiki/Kurt_Wintgens" title="Kurt Wintgens">Kurt Wintgens</a></b> became the first person to shoot down another plane in <a href="/wiki/Aerial_warfare" title="Aerial warfare">aerial combat</a> using a <a href="/wiki/Interrupter_gear" title="Interrupter gear">synchronized machine gun</a>.</li>

<li style="-moz-float-edge: content-box"><a href="/wiki/1943" title="1943">1943</a> <b><a href="/wiki/Tokyo_City" title="Tokyo City">Tokyo City</a></b> was dissolved, with its territory divided into the <a href="/wiki/Special_wards_of_Tokyo" title="Special wards of Tokyo">special wards</a> of the newly created <b><a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo Metropolis</a></b>.</li>
<li style="-moz-float-edge: content-box"><a href="/wiki/2006" title="2006">2006</a> The <b><a href="/wiki/Qinghai%E2%80%93Tibet_Railway" title="QinghaiTibet Railway">QinghaiTibet Railway</a></b>, the only railway line to the <a href="/wiki/Tibet_Autonomous_Region" title="Tibet Autonomous Region">Tibet Autonomous Region</a>, was inaugurated.</li>
